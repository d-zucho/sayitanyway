# SayItAnyway — Social Media Strategy

## Brand Voice

**The short version:** Unapologetically progressive. Evidence-obsessed. No hedging.

SayItAnyway has a point of view — left-leaning — and doesn't pretend otherwise. But the edge that makes it different is that the takes are *backed*. Not just vibes, not just rage. You call things out and you show your receipts.

### Voice Pillars

| Quality | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| **Direct** | Say the thing. Don't bury the lede in qualifiers. |
| **Sharp** | Wit over snark. Clever beats mean. |
| **Rigorous** | Claims come with sources. Always. |
| **Accessible** | Wonky ideas, plain language. No jargon without explanation. |
| **Human** | You're a person with opinions, not a press release. |

### What SayItAnyway is NOT

- Not neutral. You have a perspective; own it.
- Not a rage account. Anger is a tool, not a personality.
- Not above accountability — you'll fact-check bad takes on *any* side, including the left.
- Not academic. This is a blog, not a dissertation.

### Voice in practice

**Do:** "The claim that [X] is actually false — here's what the data shows."
**Don't:** "Some people say… others argue… it's complicated."

**Do:** "This policy would hurt working people. Here's why."
**Don't:** "There are many perspectives on this policy."

## Platforms

### X / Twitter — Your Primary Growth Engine

X is where political discourse lives in real time. This is where you build credibility, find your audience, and drive traffic to the blog.

**What works on X:**
- Fast, hot takes on breaking news
- Fact-check threads (your core product)
- Quote-tweets with corrections
- Personal opinions stated plainly
- Engaging with/correcting accounts that spread misinformation

**What to avoid:**
- Posting and disappearing — reply, engage, be in the conversation
- Long blocks of text with no hook
- Posting the same thing you'd put on Instagram

---

### Instagram — Your Visual Layer

Instagram is where you build brand recognition and reach younger audiences who don't live on X. Think of it as the visual companion to your X presence.

**What works on Instagram:**
- Fact-check infographics (claim → verdict → 3 key facts → source)
- Carousel explainers ("The real story on [topic] — swipe →")
- Stories for quick reactions and polls
- Reels for reach (short, punchy, captioned)

**What to avoid:**
- Long captions without a strong first line (only 2 lines show before "more")
- Stock photo aesthetic — keep it bold, opinionated, designed
- Posting without a visual hook

## Content Pillars

Build every post from one of these five pillars. They give you structure without making every post feel the same.

### 1. The Fact-Check ✅
Your core product. Take a claim circulating in the news or on social media, verify it, and publish the verdict.

- **X:** Thread format. Hook tweet → breakdown → sources → verdict
- **Instagram:** Infographic carousel. Claim on slide 1, verdict + evidence on 2–4, sources on last slide

*Frequency: 3–4x per week*

### 2. The Hot Take 🔥
A clear, stated opinion on something happening in politics or culture. You're not pretending to be neutral here — this is the "SayItAnyway" part.

- **X:** 1–3 tweet post. Lead with the take, follow with why.
- **Instagram:** Bold text graphic + caption that unpacks the reasoning

*Frequency: Daily (this is your highest-volume pillar)*

### 3. The Explainer 📖
Something complicated, made simple. Great for younger audiences who want to understand an issue without reading a 4,000-word article.

- **X:** Mini-thread. "What's actually happening with [topic] — a thread 🧵"
- **Instagram:** Carousel. Each slide = one concept. 5–8 slides max.

*Frequency: 2x per week*

### 4. The Callout ⚡
A specific account, politician, outlet, or pundit said something wrong or misleading. You correct it, publicly, with receipts.

- **X:** Quote-tweet with correction, or @mention with evidence
- **Instagram:** "They said / Reality" graphic format

*Frequency: As it happens, 2–3x per week*

### 5. Behind the Post 🔍
Pull back the curtain on your research process. Show how you verify a claim, where you find sources, why you chose a verdict. Builds massive trust.

- **X:** Short thread or single tweet
- **Instagram:** Talking-head reel or story series

*Frequency: 1x per week*

## Daily Posting Framework

Daily posting is sustainable only with a system. Here's a repeatable weekly rhythm:

| Day | X | Instagram |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Hot take on weekend news | Fact-check infographic |
| Tuesday | Fact-check thread | Explainer carousel |
| Wednesday | Callout + correction | Hot take graphic |
| Thursday | Explainer thread | Behind the post reel/story |
| Friday | Hot take or fact-check | Callout graphic |
| Saturday | Engage + reply day (no new content required) | Story: poll or question |
| Sunday | Week-in-review tweet | Week-in-review story |

**Rule:** X should always post before or same-day as Instagram. X is reactive; Instagram is produced.

## Growth Tactics

### X
- Reply to accounts with 10k–100k followers in the political space (not the mega-accounts — your reply gets buried)
- Quote-tweet misinformation from viral posts — this is a discovery engine
- Use threads for your best work; single tweets for hot takes
- Post between 8–10am and 6–8pm local time for max reach

### Instagram
- Use 5–8 hashtags per post (specific beats broad: `#factcheck` beats `#politics`)
- Reels get pushed harder than carousels — aim for at least 2 reels per week
- Engage with Stories: polls, "agree or disagree" sliders drive algorithmic boost
- Collab posts with similar accounts once you have a few hundred followers

## What to Build First (Before You Post)

Before you start posting at volume, have these ready:

1. **Bio locked** — X and Instagram bios should be identical in substance: what SayItAnyway is, your stance, link to the blog (once live)
2. **Profile aesthetic** — Choose a color palette and font for Instagram graphics and stick to it. Canva works fine.
3. **5 posts ready to go** — Don't launch cold. Have 2 fact-checks, 2 hot takes, 1 explainer queued up before you hit post on anything.
4. **Link in bio** — Even if the blog isn't live, have a landing page or "coming soon" page

## Voice Consistency Checklist

Before posting anything, run through this:

- [ ] Is the take clearly stated, not buried?
- [ ] If making a factual claim, is there a source?
- [ ] Does this sound like a person, not a brand?
- [ ] Would I say this out loud?
- [ ] Is it punchy enough to stop a scroll?
